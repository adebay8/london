# Beaufort-flats area expansion — research dossier

**Date:** 2026-06-25
**Purpose:** Identify Zone 3 NW/W London areas/developments matching the "Beaufort Park archetype" to extend the `beaufort-flats` skill beyond Colindale NW9.
**Method:** `deep-research` workflow (106 agents, 24 sources fetched, 98 claims, 25 adversarially verified → 20 confirmed / 5 refuted) + targeted follow-up searches. Findings below are tagged by confidence.

## Scope locked with user
- **Anchor stays:** Beaufort Park + Colindale Gardens (NW9, Zone 4) remain the home area, unchanged.
- **New areas must be Zone 3** (Zone 2 only if 1-bed rents land ≤ £2,000). Zone 4 candidates are rejected *as new additions* even if the archetype fits.
- **Budget:** target £1,600–£1,850; **£1,851–£2,000 = "over budget" (kept but collapsed** in the viewer like discarded rows); **> £2,000 = out** (note + down-rank).
- **Two tiers, both kept.** Tier 1 = established large master-planned regeneration w/ mixed BTR+private tenure & amenities (Beaufort-like). Tier 2 = isolated/new development in a genuinely good area (ranked lower).
- **Rubric** = the app's 6 weighted criteria, commute-anchored to **Westminster**, Tube/DLR/Elizabeth ≫ National Rail: transport .25, safety .25, rentValue .20, newBuilds .15, amenities .10, areaQuality .05.

## Headline finding (the honest conclusion)
The true Beaufort archetype in Zone 3 NW/W **comes at a premium**. Most archetype-matching developments (North Acton BTR towers, Dickens Yard, Brent Cross Town) have 1-beds **at or above £2,000** — i.e. out of budget. The in-budget / over-but-ok sweet spot is narrow:
- **Grand Union (Alperton)** ~£1,950 — best-value true archetype, but Zone 3/4 boundary + weaker commute.
- **Acton Gardens (South Acton)** — strongest archetype & neighbourhood, but market 1-beds mostly £2,000+ (some lower affordable/older stock).
- **Hendon Waterside (NW9)** — cheaper (£1,500–1,900) and Beaufort-like in form, but real safety red flags + National Rail.
- **Cricklewood pockets** — in-budget stock exists (e.g. Akelius/Gerard Court £1,725–1,800) but archetype is weak.

Translation: expanding to Zone 3 mostly buys a **better-connected / more central** location, usually by **paying up to ~£2,000**, not by finding cheaper Beaufort-equivalents.
